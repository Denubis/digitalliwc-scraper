Monument
	ID:	2010
	Objekt:	sarcophagus
	Material:	marble
	Herkunft:	Roman
	Land:	CH
	Kategorie:	sarcophagus
	Name:	Bronte Nyx Ouranos Thalassa Helios/Sol Paridis iudicium Tellus Zeus/Iuppiter
	Beschreibung:	Two episodes in one scene: On the left the Judgement of Paris, on the right the return of the three goddesses to Olympos. In the left section the figure of a child, today quite damaged. It was shown frontal and rising its right hand. It is usually interpreted as one of the Winds, but it might also be Tonitrum due to its association with Caelum and Nox. On the right corner of the right scene Zeus and Selene, Nyx draped, seated towards the right (head missing). Ouranos is located below the throne of Zeus, accompanied by Okeanos and Tellus. Thalassa is reclining and seen from the back. Sol on a quadriga.
