	Name:	Achilleus/Achle Agamemnon Aias I Aias II Amphiaraos Charon I/Charu(n) Eteokles Gorgo, Gorgones Kassandra I Kerberos Nestor Phoinix II Poine Sisyphos I Vanth
	Beschreibung:	Scene interpreted as the sacrifice of the Trojan captives to Patroclus' Dii Manes. Nestor on one wall and Phoinix on another, separated by an entrance. In the frieze of animals Kerberos, two heads facing ahead, one back. Sisyphos (Sisphe) with a large stone on his shoulders, a winged daemon (Poine?) perched on it. Amphiaraos stands on a lower level, foot propped on a rock. Vanth standing between Achilleus and Agamemnon.
	Inschrift:	Nestur, Phuinis, Sisphe
