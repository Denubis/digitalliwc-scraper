	Szenerieangabe:	cryptoporticus
	Beschreibung:	Diomedes (I) and Athena in a chariot. Diomedes raises his spear against a warrior (Aineias or Ares?). Chariot to the left where two warriors exchange swords; the one on the right is named Diomedes. In the smithy of Hephaistos Euanthe (II) is sitting at the left, head inclined, seizing the shield of Achilleus. Behind her Thetis, seated and accompanied by a second female woman. River-god Skamandros seated on a rock, Achilleus and Lykaon. In another scene Briseis and Talthybios - only their lower parts and the inscriptions remain. In another scene the council of the Achaean leaders are sitting with their shields on the ground: Aias (I), Menestheus, Diomedes, Nestor and Thrasymedes. Apollo above on the left, shooting his arrows; on a chariot old man raising his arms, maybe the seer Kalchas. Neptunus, Aeneas and Achilleus.
	Bibliographie:	Schefold, WP 17-18; Spinazzola, Pompei II 904-907. 909-928. 931-941. 943. 946-950 fig. 901-903. 915-919. 923-928. 935-951. 954-963. 971; Bianchi Bandinelli, R., Hellenistic-Byzantine Minitiatures of the Iliad (1955) 119-120 fig. 175; Galinsky, G.
