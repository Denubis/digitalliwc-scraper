	Name:	Acheron Aktai Ananke Anchialos Danaides Elpenor Eurybates II Eurylochos I Kirke Laistrygones Nomai Odysseus Orion Perimedes Poine Sisyphos I Teiresias Tityos Eidola Ariadne Nekyia Phaidra Venti
	Beschreibung:	Different panels with adventures of Odysseus: Palast der Kirke von innen; Kirke öffnet Odysseus die Türe. Links von der Doppeltüre eine bärtige Hüftherme mit Armstümpfen, auf Sockel. Panel 5: Three women (inscription AKTAI) on the rim of the river seated on rocks. Three panels are dedicated to the meeting of the Greeks with the Laistryngones; one of them shows three Greek men (Anchialos, Antilochos and Eurybates) approaching the daughter of the king of the Laistyngones. Above the ships of Odysseus are three winged Venti (Winds). They refer to the story with the tube of Aiolos which the comrades of Odysseus opened while he was sleeping.  In the Underworld, with others, four Danaids near a kind of trough. Above perhaps another Danaid. Close to the entrance to hell Eurylochos (I) and Perimedes sacrificing an animal. Then Odysseus, Teiresias, Phaidra, Ariadne, Leda and maybe Antikleia. In hell Orion threatening an invisible deer with his club. Below him Sisyphos atop a cliff pushing a stone. Tityos maltreated by two vultures.

Berlin (1988) 282-283 no. 131-132; Schefold/Jung, SB V 356 fig. 318; Ling, R., Roman Painting (1991) 110 fig. 111; Biering, R., Die Odyssefresken vom Esquilin  (1995) 95-114 pl. 19-24 .
